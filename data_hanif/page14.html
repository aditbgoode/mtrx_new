Is Soybean Oil Competitive Against Palm Oil?

by Terry Roggensack on April 21, 2014 in Commentary

Soybean oil continues to find increased support from El Nino talk and from solid gains in palm oil during the past few weeks. Soybean oil’s premium to palm oil at Rotterdam is close to its lowest level since 2006, when palm oil traded at a slight premium to soybean oil. This suggests stronger demand for soybean oil ahead. A dry weather outlook for Indonesia and Malaysia continues to send signals that palm oil production might be on the downtrend over the long term. This situation needs to be monitored closely, as the world vegetable oil market is dominated by palm oil exports from Malaysia and Indonesia. In terms of tonnage, palm oil exports from Malaysia and Indonesia run about seven times those for soybean oil from Argentina and the US. The El Nino impact is significant in Australia, and a strong El Nino can also cause dryness into Southeast Asia. India is a major vegetable oil consumer, and traders will monitor their monsoons into June, as any weather issues for oilseeds in India would spark active import demand. Minor increases in per-capita consumption of vegetable oil in India and China have caused a surge in total usage for those countries. Any weather issues in Southeast Asia that would affect the palm oil crop could quickly supportsoybean oil. In most of Europe and Asia, the price of natural gas/LNG is typically linked to crude oil under multiyear contracts. So while the spike in Brent helped to boost natural gas markers elsewhere, with practically zero LNG export capacity in the U.S., not even Fed's two rounds of quantitative easing could lift the languishing Henry Hub. Commercial interest in owning warehouse receipts for soybean oil seems to be on the rise. There were 132 cancellations of deliverable registrations of soybean oil at Danville, Illinois on Thursday, April 17th. Increased domestic bio-fuel blend rates are positive force as well. Trend-following fund traders still held a net short position of 2,208 contracts in the most recent Commitments of Traders Report, so there is plenty of potential buying support from fund tradersif the overall trend turns up. Realistically, U.S. gas cargos may have a hard time competing with other exporters such as Russia, Qatar and the up-andcomer—Australia--in the Asian and European markets due to logistic disadvantage.
